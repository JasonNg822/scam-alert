What is an Investment Scam?


Investment scams involve fraudsters luring victims with promises of high returns and minimal risk. They often promote fake or exaggerated investment opportunities—such as cryptocurrency, forex trading, or private equity—to deceive people and steal their money.



Common Tactics

  1. False promises: Claims like “guaranteed profit” or “double your money fast” prey on greed.
  2. Fake credentials: Scammers may pose as licensed financial advisors or use forged documents and websites to appear credible.
  3. Manipulated platforms: Some scams use fake trading apps or websites where they control prices. Once a large sum is invested, the platform shuts down or the scammer disappears.


If You Fall Victim

  • Contact your bank immediately.

    Provide the transfer time, amount, and recipient account to request a stop-payment or freeze. Some banks allow reversals within 24 hours.

  • Cease communication.

    Do not transfer more money or share verification codes. Block the scammer’s contacts.

  • Preserve all records:

    chat logs, transaction receipts, screenshots, or emails.
