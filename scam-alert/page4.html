What Are E-Commerce Scams?

E-commerce scams involve fake online sellers or platforms that deceive people into paying for goods or services that are never delivered. These scams often appear on social media, fake websites, or online marketplaces with too-good-to-be-true prices or limited-time offers. Once payment is made, the scammer disappears or blocks further contact.



Common Tactics

  • Fake storefronts or social media ads offering heavily discounted products.
  • Sellers who insist on payment through non-traceable methods (e.g., PayNow to personal accounts, cryptocurrency, gift cards).
  • Using fake reviews and images stolen from legitimate sources to appear trustworthy.


What to Do If You Fall Victim

  • Contact your bank immediately to report the transaction and attempt a reversal.
  • Report the scam to the platform (e.g., Facebook, Carousell) and block the scammer.
  • Preserve all transaction records, chat history, and screenshots, and file a police report as soon as possible.
