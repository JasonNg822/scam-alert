How to Avoid Form

What is impersonation scam

It refers to the criminal act where fraudsters assume the identities of others or institutions, using channels such as phone calls, text messages, and the internet, to fabricate false information to deceive others' trust and then carry out financial fraud. Common methods include impersonating the police and courts, customer service representatives, relatives and friends, government departments, etc. They use reasons such as investigation of the case, refund and compensation, urgent assistance, policy subsidies, etc. to induce victims to transfer money, provide bank card information, or download fraud software, thereby exploiting people's fear, greed, or gullibility to achieve the goal of fraud.



Solution for avoiding the scam

Be vigilant against identity fraud

  1. The police, courts, tax authorities and other institutions will never ask for bank transfers, verification or bank card information over the phone or text message. If you receive such a call, immediately hang up and call the official hotline to verify
  2. The official customer service of e-commerce and banks will not call or send text messages to inform customers about "refunds due to product quality issues" or "need to cancel membership due to incorrect operation and it requires confirmation through the app or the official website
  3. When receiving requests for help from "relatives or friends" via social media, text messages or unknown numbers (such as "sudden illness / car accident requiring remittance"), always verify the information by calling, videoing or directly contacting the person concerned. Avoid making transfers based solely on verbal communication


Measures of remediation

  • Immediately call the police and provide as much detail as possible regarding the fraud, such as the accent of the perpetrator, the phone number used, the account number, etc.
  • Preserve evidence: Collect and retain all evidence related to the fraud, such as chat records, call records, transfer records, transaction vouchers, links or QR codes sent by the perpetrator, etc.
