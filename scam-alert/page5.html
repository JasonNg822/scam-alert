How to Avoid Form

Views about scam:

Scam is a serious criminal activity that violates both legal and moral standards. It involves deceiving and misleading others to obtain their property or information, causing great harm to people's financial security, mental health, and even social order.

The characteristic of scam is the coexistence of concealment and diversity. As its methods improve, many people will be deceived and it will become increasingly difficult to manage.

How to Protect Yourself


Impersonation Scam

  • Authorities (police, courts, tax departments) will never request bank details or transfers via calls or messages. Always verify with official hotlines.
  • Legitimate customer service from banks or e-commerce platforms won't contact you about refunds or account cancellations requiring app downloads or confirmations.
  • If a “friend” or “relative” requests urgent help via social media or unknown numbers, confirm directly through a phone or video call before acting.


Investment Scam

  • ADD: Set privacy controls on messaging apps to avoid unknown group invites. Enable 2FA (Two-Factor Authentication) on banking and financial apps. Use transaction limits and money lock features (e.g., on PayNow, PayLah, and bank accounts) to protect your funds.
  • CHECK: Consult a trusted person before making any financial decision. Call the ScamShield Helpline at 1799 if you're unsure about an investment.
  • TELL: Warn friends and family about scams you encounter. Report and block suspicious accounts or chat groups. Contact your bank and file a police report if you believe you've been scammed.

E-Commerce Scams

  • Only shop on reputable platforms or websites with secure payment options (look for “https://”).
  • Avoid sellers who pressure you to pay quickly or outside the platform.
  • Search for reviews and complaints before making a purchase, and verify with ScamShield or official channels.
  • Never share banking details or OTPs with sellers or third parties.
